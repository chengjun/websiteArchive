About Me

Cheng-Jun Wang is currently an assistant research fellow in the School of Journalism and Communication, Nanjing University. He is the director of Ogilvy Data Science Lab, and he is also a research member of Computational Communication Collaboratory and Web Mining Lab. His research on computational communication appears in both SSCI and SCI indexed journals, such as Scientific Reports, PloS ONE, Physica A, Cyberpsychology. In 2014, He founded the website of computational communication. You can find his CV here.

The Collective Direction of Attention Diffusion

We suggest that the tree-like, stable structure of clickstream networks reveals the time-sensitive preference of users in online browsing. To test our assumption, we discuss three models on individual browsing behavior, and compare the simulation results with empirical data.
Cheng-Jun Wang, Lingfei Wu, Jiang Zhang, Marco A. Janssen
